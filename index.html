Community Work Centre Induction

Overview

The Community Work Centre induction continues to build on what has been discussed at the initial assessment and provides another opportunity to demonstrate manaakitanga and establish the requirements and expectations of the sentence. It is important to clearly establish the working relationship with the community worker and set the reporting frequency to ensure the sentence is successfully completed.

Required Actions

  • Review file, including IOMS information. Verify information with the community worker and tailor the interaction to best suit the person you are working with.
  • Complete the Community Work Centre Induction, including the CW Centre Induction Confirmation Form.
  • Establish a reporting regime and issue an instruction to report that will meet the requirement of completing 100 hours within a 6 month timeframe. Standard of Practice: Schedule contact â€“ an instruction to report is in IOMS

Key Decisions

Establishing a Reporting Regime

Where possible, we should ensure a person can successfully complete their hours while also maintaining their employment and family responsibilities. It is important to balance the needs of the community worker and what is available and practical within your operational constraints. Much of this information will be contained within the Community Work Placement Assessment and WLS Suitability form.


There will be some situations where this is a challenge and will require some negotiation with the community worker. If they are unwilling to commit to an instruction to report that will ensure they meet the legislative requirements of their sentence, you should consider an application to cancel the sentence.


Considerations

Sentence and risk factors:
  • Are there alerts or other risk indicators to be considered? (eg gang affiliation, non-associations or co-offenders completing community work)
  • Do they have a concerning previous offending history? (take particular note of previous violence or sexual offending)
  • How long is the current sentence? What hours are available to ensure completion within the maximum completion timeframe?
  • Do they want to complete hours quickly?
Personal factors:
  • Are they young and / or vulnerable (physical / mental / intellectual needs, gender needs, language barriers or victim of offending)? How can we ensure their safety during community work?
  • Do they have skill sets that link to specific projects being completed?
Availability factors
  • Do they have employment or family / whanau responsibilities that will limit their availability to complete hours?
  • Are there cultural or religions factors that need to be considered? language
  • Do they require transport support to attend community work?
  • Are they on the methadone programme?

Possible Actions

  • Induction may be completed in a group or individual setting and can include the induction video.
  • Induction may include additional site specific information such as:
    • health and safety requirements and/or tool safety briefings
    • transport options and pick up points
    • explanation of daily muster process
    • WLS or employment support opportunities available.
  • Induction may also include WLS component such as a literacy or numeracy assessment, engagement with the local education and employment staff or another WLS sessions.
  • Where required, induction should include the management of medications and cultural or religious beliefs that will need to be supported whilst completing community work.
